How Suppression Works

Understanding the unsubscribe flow and what happens behind the scenes.

⚠️ Important: The suppression list does NOT improve spam placement. Spam filters check SPF/DKIM/DMARC, sender reputation, and engagement — not unsubscribe links. This feature is about compliance and respecting recipient choice.

What happens when someone is suppressed?

  • The email is blocked from ALL future bulk sends
  • The lead gets a unsubscribed badge in the lead list
  • Individual sends to that lead are also blocked with a warning
  • The suppression is permanent unless you manually remove it
  • The count in the sidebar badge updates automatically

Ways someone gets added to the suppression list

MethodDescription
Link clickRecipient clicks "Unsubscribe" in email footer and confirms on the landing page
ManualYou manually add an email (e.g., they replied "stop" to you)
BounceHard bounces can optionally be auto-added (configurable in Settings)
💡 Why a simple one-click unsubscribe? For cold outreach, a simple one-click unsubscribe is better than a preference center. Preference centers add complexity and signal you're a bulk sender. A clear opt-out looks more like a personal email.
